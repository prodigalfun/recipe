The Pineapple Pie Recipe

Background

The Pineapple Pie is a staple cuisine. Ideal for family gatherings, events or a personal desert for anyone who appreciates
the pineapple fruit for the masterpiece it is. Seriously, give this recipe a whirl. It's quite beginner friendly, not to mention very
nourishing. The pineapple toppings helps offset the unhealthiness of the common pie, which are often caked in sugar and little else.
This, however, is a refreshing change of pace.

Ingredients

1 (20 ounce) can crushed pineapple with juice

Â¾ cup white sugar

3 tablespoons cornstarch

1 tablespoon lemon juice

1 (14.1 ounce) package double-crust pie pastry, thawed

2 tablespoons milk

1 tablespoon white sugar

Directions

Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).

Combine crushed pineapple with juice, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice in a medium saucepan.
Cook and stir over medium heat until thickened, then boil for 1 minute. Allow filling to cool slightly.

Line a 9-inch pie dish with 1 crust. Pour filling into the prepared pie dish. Cover with remaining crust;
press and flute the edges to seal. Cut a few steam vents on top, brush with milk, and sprinkle with sugar.

Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 35 minutes. Serve chilled or at room temperature.
